Zeus

King of the Gods and ruler of Mount Olympus. The roar of his thunder strikes fear amongst mortals and gods alike.

Symbols

  • Lightning Bolt
  • Oak Tree
  • Bull

Poseidon

The Earth Shaker. Every crashing wave and tumbling river are his dominion.

Symbols

  • Trident
  • Horse
  • Dolphin

Hermes

The Messenger of the Gods. Clever and mischievous, he is also the patron god of tricksters and thieves.

Symbols

  • Talaria(Winged Sandals)
  • Tortoise
  • Goat

Ares

The God of War. Bloodthirsty and excitable, he is disliked by the other gods.

Symbols

  • Spear
  • Helmet
  • Vulture

Hephaestus

He is the God of fire and the forge. A master craftsmen, he has created the finest weapons and armor.

Symbols

  • Hammer
  • Anvil
  • Tongs

Dionysus

The God of Wine and Festivity. He is depicted as a joyous and festive man.

Symbols

  • Thyrsus(Ornamental Spear)
  • Chalice
  • Grapevine

Apollo

The God of Archery, Music, Prophecy, and Disease. It is said that he has never missed a shot.

Symbols

  • Lyre
  • Raven
  • Laurel Wreath

Artemis

The Goddess of the Hunt and the Eternal Virgin. She was also the protector of young women.

Symbols

  • Moon
  • Bow
  • Arrow

Athena

The Goddess of Wisdom and War. She is strategic and methodical.

Symbols

  • Owl
  • Olive
  • Snake

Aphrodite

The Goddess of Beauty and Lust. Born from sea foam created upon Uranus' death.

Symbols

  • Rose
  • Dove
  • Sparrow

Demeter

The Goddess of the Harvest and Agriculture. The seasons change in accordance with her mood.

Symbols

  • Torch
  • Cornucopia
  • Loaf of Bread

Hera

Queen of the gods. Despite Zeus's unfaithfulness, she remains unwaveringly by his side.

Symbols

  • Diadem(Tiara)
  • Peacock
  • Pomegranate
