Zeus

King of the Gods and ruler of Mount Olympus. The roar of his thunder strikes fear among mortals. His symbols are a thunderbolt, a bull, an eagle, and an oak tree.

Poseidon

The Earth Shaker. Every crashing wave and tumbling river are his dominion. His symbols are a trident, a bull, a horse, a dolphin, and a fish.

Hermes

The Messenger of the Gods. Clever and mischievous, he is also the patron god of tricksters and thieves. His symbols include a tortoise, a talaria(winged sandals), and a goat.

Ares

The God of War. Bloodthirsty and excitable, he is disliked by the other gods. His symbols include a spear, a helmet, and a vulture.

Hephaestus

He is the God of fire and the forge. A master craftsmen, he has created the finest weapons and armor. His symbols include a hammer, an anvil, and a pair of tongs.

Dionysus

The God of Wine and Festivity. He is depicted as a joyous and festive man. His symbols include a thyrsus, a grapevine, and a chalice.

Apollo

The God of Archery, Music, Prophecy, and Disease. It is said that he has never missed a shot. His symbols include a lyre, a python, a raven, a laurel wreath, and a bow and arrow.

Artemis

The Goddess of the Hunt and the Eternal Virgin. She was also the protector of young women. Her symbols included the moon and a bow and arrow.

Athena

The Goddess of Wisdom and War. She is strategic and methodical. Her symbols include an owl, an olive, and a snake.

Aphrodite

The Goddess of Beauty and Lust. Born from sea foam created upon Uranus' death. Her symbols include a rose, a dove, and a sparrow.

Demeter

The Goddess of the Harvest and Agriculture. The seasons change in accordance with her mood. Her symbols include a torch, a cornucopia, and a loaf of bread.

Hera

Queen of the gods. Despite Zeus's unfaithfulness, she remains unwaveringly by his side. Her symbols include a diadem, a peacock, and a pomegranate.
